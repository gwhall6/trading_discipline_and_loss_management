## My Core Market Philosophy

**Newton's first law applies to markets.** Absent a change in force, the market continues in its current direction. The market is right until it proves otherwise. I trade with that, not against it.

**The long-term bias is up.** As civilization progresses and productivity compounds, wealth is created and the market reflects that. My fundamental edge is to be a long trader. I will trade short selectively, but long is my default posture.

**The higher timeframe sets the direction. The lower timeframe sets the entry.** The opportunity comes when a lower timeframe trend temporarily disagrees with the higher timeframe trend — and then realigns. That realignment is where I make money.

**Two structural edges I want in my favor:**
1. Trading in the direction of the longer-term trend — long bias
2. Selling option premium — time decay works for me, not against me

---

## Market Regime — Current Snapshot
*Last updated: March 2026 — revisit when regime changes*

**Regime: Elevated volatility with long bias**

The dominant force driving volatility right now is policy uncertainty under the current administration. Expect continued headline-driven swings in both directions.

At the same time, there is a personal and political incentive for the market to move higher. History in this environment suggests that aggressive moves down tend to be walked back — what traders call the TACO trade: Trump Always Chickens Out. Tariff threats, geopolitical escalation, and market selloffs have repeatedly been followed by softening rhetoric and relief rallies.

**What this means for my trading:**
- Buy-the-dip setups are favored in this regime until the market proves otherwise
- Take profits when profits are available — do not hold through volatility expecting continuation
- On down days, look for the level where real buyers step in — that is where the opportunity is
- Remain alert for a genuine regime change: sustained breakdown of key levels with no recovery would signal the TACO assumption is no longer valid

## Section 1: The 1pm Market & Watchlist Review

### Market Read — QQQ
Review three timeframes: Daily, 65-minute, 2-minute.

Identify and mark:
- Levels where price failed or was rejected today
- Levels where price held and bounced
- Levels price broke through and whether it reclaimed or not
- Any after-hours or pre-market price movement relative to those levels

Write one game plan note directly on the chart in this format:

> *If price breaks and holds above [level], look for long entries on retests of broken resistance. If price breaks below [level], look for shorts as price holds below.*

The game plan is conditional — it does not predict direction, it defines the response to each scenario.

Post QQQ game plan to SMB training Discord.

---

### Watchlist Review
Review charts for each ticker on the watchlist (target: 8-10 tickers max).

For each ticker, mark key levels on all three timeframes and answer:
1. Is price near a level I care about?
2. Did today's action change or confirm that level?
3. Could tomorrow produce my setup?

If yes to #3, write a game plan note on the chart and set alerts at the key levels.

Remove tickers where the thesis is dead or stale. Add tickers from SMB commentary only after higher timeframe review — not same day.

---

### Entry Signal (what I am waiting for)
All four conditions required — no exceptions:

1. **Break** — price breaks through a significant level with conviction
2. **Retest** — price returns to test that broken level
3. **Consolidation** — price holds and consolidates near that level
4. **Volume confirmation** — price breaks higher again on elevated volume; the volume candle visibly stands out relative to surrounding candles on the 2-minute chart

This is the signal. Anything less is not the signal.

---

### Profit Taking
Scale out in three equal pieces:

- **1st third — exit at 1:1** — locks in a win immediately, reduces risk on remaining position
- **2nd third — exit at 1.5:1 normally; exit at 2:1 if price has extended away from the 5 EMA** — the extension condition is a momentum read, not an arbitrary target
- **Final third — trail until 5:1 is reached, or until structure breaks** — whichever comes first

*Structure break definition — to be defined after observation*
Candidates:
- Close below 5 EMA on the 2-minute chart
- Break of the most recent higher low
- Both — whichever comes first

Do not hold the final third through a structural reason to exit hoping for 5:1. The trail is a gift, not an entitlement.

## Section 2: The 5:30am Morning Prep

**5:30–6:00am — 30 minutes, no exceptions**

1. **Update charts** — review watchlist tickers, check if any levels were hit overnight or in pre-market
2. **Lock in notes** — confirm or adjust game plan notes from the 1pm review based on overnight price action
3. **Build the game plan spreadsheet** — one row per ticker with: level, entry trigger, target, invalidation point, setup rating, % of daily stop allocated, planned stop level, number of shares. Shares and stop % are formulas — not estimates.
4. **RVOL check** — scan watchlist tickers for elevated relative volume pre-market. Elevated RVOL = ticker stays active. Low RVOL = deprioritize.
5. **Attend SMB morning meeting (6:00–6:30)** — filter Steve's commentary through one question: does this confirm something already on my spreadsheet? If yes, note it. If no, it goes into the post-market review bucket — it does not become a same-day trade.

---

### Game Plan Spreadsheet — Required Fields Per Ticker
| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Ticker | |
| Level | The specific price level being watched |
| Entry trigger | Break / retest / consolidate / volume confirmation |
| Target | Next resistance level identified before entry |
| Invalidation | Where the trade is structurally wrong |
| Setup rating | Quality score — determines max allocation |
| % of daily stop | How much of the $50 daily limit this trade can use |
| Planned stop price | Specific price, not a feeling |
| Share count | Formula: (daily stop × % allocated) ÷ (entry − stop) |

---

### The Two Buckets Rule
**Bucket 1 — Active trades:** Tickers on the spreadsheet with all fields complete. These are the only trades available today.

**Bucket 2 — Review queue:** Anything surfaced in the SMB meeting, during the trading hour, or from any other source that isn't already on the spreadsheet. Reviewed at 1pm, not traded same day.

---

### On Trades I'm Not In
There will be many trades I am not in. That is the system working correctly.

- A trade I'm not in because the entry has passed is not a missed opportunity — it is a trade that didn't meet my criteria at the right time
- I review levels during my 1pm prep and identify where an optimal entry would have been, where price is now, and at what level it would make sense to re-enter
- I set an alert at that level and wait — I do not chase
- Getting into a trade without a perfect entry is valid if the risk/reward is still good from current price to my stop and target
- Letting a trade go that no longer has good risk/reward is also correct, even if I believe in the direction

## Section 3: The Trading Hour (6:30–7:30am)

### The Alert-Driven Process
I do not watch price action looking for trades. I wait for alerts to trigger.

1. **Alert triggers** — I look at my game plan document
2. **Setup confirms** — I type in my stop level and share count from the spreadsheet
3. **I enter** — with a hard stop already in the market before the order fills
4. **I follow the profit-taking rules** — exactly as written

### Non-Negotiable Rules
- **Hard stops always** — in the market, not mental, before or immediately at entry
- **Never trade anything not written down and posted before the market opened** — no exceptions
- **If I read about something or see something moving during the trading hour** — it goes in the bucket, it does not get traded today
- **I do not look for new setups during the trading hour** — my job from 6:30–7:30 is execution, not analysis

### The Commitment
These rules exist because I understand exactly how and why I've failed before. They are not restrictions — they are the system working. The moment I make an exception is the moment I'm no longer trading this plan.
